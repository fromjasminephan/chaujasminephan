WEVOLV Salary Benchmarking System

Northeastern University ALY6980  ·  Sponsor: WEVOLV  ·  Python · Ridge/Lasso · PostgreSQL

7,977contract records
R² 0.839Ridge & Lasso
60cohort cells

My contribution

I led Ridge and Lasso regression modeling and built the cohort quantile engine computing P25/P50/P75 salary within 60 peer groups (3 tiers × 5 positions × 4 experience brackets). I also discovered that 50.9% of raw user accounts were duplicate player profiles — a data governance finding resolved using a composite identity key.

Key findings

Market tier membership accounts for 88.1% of model gain. The fair range intersection logic classified 48.9% of contracts as FAIR, 25.7% UNDERPAID, and 25.4% OVERPAID across all 7,977 records.

The problem

International basketball players outside the NBA negotiate contracts with no reliable salary benchmarks. WEVOLV needed an engine to answer: "For someone like you, where does this offer fall relative to the market?"

Salary Transparency Index — International Basketball

Northeastern University ALY6980  ·  Python · Random Forest · Ridge Regression

7,867salary records
R² 0.728Random Forest
$5.6Mmarket opportunity

What I built

A 4-dimension Salary Transparency Index scoring 8 major international leagues. The NBA scored 99.7/100; CBA scored 3.5/100 — a 96-point gap. A Random Forest on 7,867 records found position accounts for 61.6% of salary variance and nationality just 2.8%.

Key findings

Leagues with player unions score ~51 points higher (t=4.35, p=.005). Revenue correlates strongly with transparency (r=.80, p=.017). The EuroLeague and CBA represent a combined $5.6M annual revenue opportunity.

The problem

International basketball players negotiate contracts in markets with wildly uneven information access. This opacity enables chronic underpayment, especially for players from developing economies.

German Bank Credit Risk & Customer Segmentation

Northeastern University ALY6080  ·  Professor Bedayat  ·  Python · K-Means · SVM

1,000applicant records
3customer segments
79%SVM accuracy

What we built

K-means clustering (k=3, silhouette 0.212) segmented 1,000 German bank customers into three borrower profiles. PCA retained 90% of variance. An SVM classifier with a linear kernel achieved 79% accuracy — up from 49% with RBF — predicting Low, Medium, and High default risk.

Key findings

Three profiles emerged: asset-heavy mature borrowers (avg 6,342 DM), young independent starters (avg 2,584 DM), and balanced mid-age homeowners. Savings and checking account status showed the strongest negative correlation with default risk.

The problem

Banks struggle to evaluate loan applicants with no prior credit history. Can behavioral and demographic features alone predict default risk and enable tailored lending strategies?

Predicting National Happiness — World Happiness Report

Northeastern University ALY-6040  ·  Professor O'Farrell  ·  R · Random Forest · XGBoost

3country clusters
0.575best AUC (RF)
4models compared

What I built

Four analytical phases: K-means (k=3) segmented countries into developing, emerging, and developed tiers. Linear regression revealed non-linearity. Random Forest identified Unemployment Rate and Internet Access as top predictors. XGBoost ranked Political Stability first (12.57% gain) above GDP per Capita.

Key findings

No single variable explains national happiness. Internet Access appeared in both RF and XGBoost top 5 — an underappreciated policy lever. All clusters dipped post-2020, consistent with COVID-19 disruption.

The problem

What socioeconomic factors most significantly predict national happiness, and how can governments prioritize interventions to improve citizen wellbeing?
