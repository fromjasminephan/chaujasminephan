Overview

Project Background

This group project analyzed a large retail sales dataset (D24) for a warehouse-style store, covering transactions across multiple geographic regions, brands, and product categories. As part of ALY 6070, the team cleaned, enriched, and visualized the data to uncover actionable insights for inventory and sales strategy.

Working as part of Team Cole N. Knaflic alongside Chujie Feng, Tsz Chi Leung, and Chenye Yin, I contributed to data preparation, Python-based analysis, correlation studies, and the brand-level refund visualization.

Python Tableau Pandas Matplotlib Chi-Square Testing Cramér's V Retail Analytics
Methodology

Approach & Process

  • Data Cleaning & Enrichment Standardized date formats, engineered three new features — BRAND, CATEGORY, and Owner — extracted from item description columns and supplemented by public brand data.
  • Exploratory Analysis (Part 1) Correlation heatmap, time-series sales trend analysis, and regional revenue breakdown using Python and Tableau visualizations.
  • Brand & Geographic Analysis (Part 2) Chi-square tests + Cramér's V to assess brand-geography associations across STATE, CITY, DISTRICT, and LCNAME. Dashboard built in Tableau with treemap + map views.
  • Inventory Scoring Model Multi-factor removal score calculated per item: weighted scores for return rate (20%–50% threshold), unit price, and sales quantity — visualized in a dual-axis Tableau bar chart.
Key Findings

What the Data Revealed

Sales & Quantity Correlation

Strong positive correlation (r = 0.79) between sales quantity and revenue — expected in retail, but confirmed data integrity for downstream analysis.

Seasonal Peak — Nov 2018

A sharp spike in November 2018 aligned with Black Friday/holiday promotions, followed by a smaller February uptick from post-holiday campaigns.

EC Region Dominates

The EC region generated $200M+ in revenue, significantly outpacing BD, TE, BA, and SD — pointing to major demographic and purchasing power differences.

Top 3 Brands = 96% of Sales

HP, DELL, and LENOVO collectively captured 96%+ of sales volume while maintaining low refund rates (13.5%, 14.0%, 15.0%). ASUS and ACER showed 20%+ refund rates despite lower volume.

Brand-Geography: Weak Effect

Chi-square tests found statistically significant but practically weak correlations (Cramér's V: 0.04–0.065), meaning brand preference is relatively consistent across regions.

5 Items Recommended for Removal

Structured scoring across return rate, unit price, and sales quantity identified HP, DELL, and LENOVO items that underperformed on multiple dimensions simultaneously.
