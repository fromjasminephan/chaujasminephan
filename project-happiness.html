Business question

What socioeconomic factors most significantly predict national happiness scores, and how can governments prioritize interventions to improve citizen wellbeing? This project applied a full data mining pipeline to the World Happiness Report dataset to answer that question across multiple countries and years.

What I built

  • Phase 1: K-means clustering (k=3 via elbow method) — 3 country tiers
  • Phase 2: Linear regression — revealed non-linearity and poor fit
  • Phase 3: Random Forest — identified top socioeconomic predictors
  • Phase 4: XGBoost — ranked Political Stability #1 (12.57% gain), surprising conventional wisdom
  • Time series analysis of happiness trends 2015–2023 including COVID-19 impact

Country clusters

  • Cluster 1 — Emerging Economies: Medium GDP, moderate happiness (~5.0–6.2). Priority: social infrastructure investment.
  • Cluster 2 — Developed Nations: High GDP, high happiness (~6.5–8.0). Priority: maintain existing systems.
  • Cluster 3 — Developing Nations: Low GDP, low happiness (~4.0–5.0). Priority: economic growth. Fastest post-COVID recovery.

Key findings

  • Political Stability ranked #1 in XGBoost (12.57% gain) — above GDP per Capita
  • Internet Access appeared in both RF and XGBoost top 5 — underappreciated policy lever
  • Life Satisfaction ranked dead last (1.42% gain) — collinear with target variable
  • All models showed limited AUC (best 0.575) — happiness is genuinely complex
  • Post-2020 dip visible in all clusters — consistent with COVID-19 disruption
  • All VIF scores below 5 — no multicollinearity issues in the feature set
