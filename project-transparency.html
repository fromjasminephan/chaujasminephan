Business problem

International basketball players negotiate contracts in markets with wildly uneven information access. The NBA publishes every contract publicly; the Chinese Basketball Association publishes almost none. This opacity enables chronic underpayment, especially for players from developing economies who negotiate without agents or salary benchmarks.

What I built

  • A 4-dimension Salary Transparency Index (database availability 40%, media reporting 30%, policy accessibility 20%, financial disclosure 10%)
  • Scored 8 major leagues: NBA 99.7/100 vs CBA 3.5/100 — a 96-point gap
  • Random Forest on 7,867 records to quantify salary determinants
  • Market opportunity matrix identifying EuroLeague + CBA as $5.6M opportunity

Analytical approach

  • Phase 1: Systematic audit of 8 leagues across 4 dimensions
  • Phase 2: Descriptive stats, hypothesis testing (t-tests, ANOVA, chi-square), predictive modeling
  • Phase 3: Market opportunity matrix using Baker et al. (2021) 20% benefit estimate
  • Sensitivity analysis tested 4 weighting schemes — NBA #1 and CBA #8 held invariant
  • Nationality equity analysis via counterfactual prediction (is_US flag)

Key findings

  • Leagues with player unions score ~51 points higher (t=4.35, p=.005)
  • Revenue correlates strongly with transparency (r=.80, p=.017)
  • Position accounts for 61.6% of salary variance; nationality just 2.8%
  • US/non-US salary gap disappears once tier is controlled — a tier-distribution artifact
  • EuroLeague + CBA = $5.6M annual revenue opportunity at 7.5% platform capture
